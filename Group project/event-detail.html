Model UN Conference 2026

Academic · March 25, 2026 · Sherubtse Hall

Academic

About This Event

The Model United Nations (MUN) Conference 2026 is one of Sherubtse College's most prestigious academic events. Students from all departments participate as delegates representing countries from across the globe, engaging in intense debates on pressing world issues.

This immersive simulation helps students develop critical thinking, public speaking, research skills, and an understanding of international diplomacy. Facilitated by faculty advisors and senior student moderators, the conference spans two full days of committee sessions, resolution drafting, and formal debate.

What to Expect

  • Opening Ceremony with distinguished guests from academia and government
  • Committee sessions covering Security Council, General Assembly, and specialized bodies
  • Position paper preparation and formal debate rounds
  • Resolution drafting, voting, and closing ceremony
  • Best Delegate and Outstanding Delegate awards
  • Networking dinner and cultural showcase

Who Can Participate?

All Sherubtse College students are eligible to participate. No prior MUN experience is required — training sessions will be held in the two weeks leading up to the conference. Students are encouraged to register early as seats are limited to 120 delegates.

Schedule at a Glance

  • March 20–24: Training sessions (6:00 PM, Seminar Hall)
  • March 25, 8:00 AM: Opening Ceremony
  • March 25, 10:00 AM–5:00 PM: Committee Sessions Day 1
  • March 26, 9:00 AM–4:00 PM: Committee Sessions Day 2
  • March 26, 5:00 PM: Closing Ceremony & Awards
